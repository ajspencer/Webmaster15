Regulation of Centromere Function

Centromere function is essential for proper cell division, but the mechanism for determining the formation and location of the centromere is not well understood. Centromeres are a paradox because, while their basic function is very similar among eukaryotes, centromeric DNA sequences are different, even between related species. In this project, Alyssa Ferris examines the forces behind centromeres' regulation.

Optimizing Synthesis of Flat-Sheet Phase Inversion Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF) Membranes

Accompanying the world's ever-growing population is an increased demand for efficient and environmentally friendly means of producing portable water. Membrane distillation (MD) is a rising technology combining distillation and filtration techniques that seeks to address this issue. In this project, Margaret Pan aims to optimize the synthesis of porous polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) membranes for MD by evaluation the synergistic effect of various synthesis parameters on membrane morphology.
